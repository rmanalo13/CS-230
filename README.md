# The Gaming Room Design
# Project Overview
The client, The Gaming Room, is a company seeking to expand their existing Android-based game, Draw It or Lose It, into a web-based application. The goal was to design a scalable, cross-platform system that allows multiple teams and players to participate in a timed image-guessing game. Key requirements included supporting multiple concurrent users, ensuring unique game and team names, and maintaining a consistent experience across different operating systems.

# What I Did Well
I was particularly effective in organizing the design document in a clear and structured way. I focused on breaking down system requirements, constraints, and architecture into sections that are easy for both technical and non-technical stakeholders to understand. I also did well in explaining design choices, such as platform considerations and the use of object-oriented principles.

# What Helped in the Design Process
Working through the design document helped me better understand the importance of planning before coding. It allowed me to think through system architecture, potential constraints, and scalability concerns early on. This made the development process more efficient and reduced the likelihood of major issues later in implementation.

# What I Would Revise
If I could revise one part of my work, I would improve the evaluation of different platform options. I would include more detailed comparisons of operating systems and technologies, along with stronger justification for the final recommendation. Adding more technical depth would make the document even more effective.

# Interpreting User Needs
I interpreted the user’s needs by focusing on usability, accessibility, and scalability. For example, ensuring that the game could support multiple players and teams simultaneously was critical to meeting the client’s requirements. Considering user needs is essential because it ensures the final product is functional, intuitive, and aligned with business goals.

# My Approach to Software Design
I approached the design by analyzing requirements first, then applying object-oriented design principles to structure the system. I also considered scalability, platform compatibility, and maintainability throughout the process. In the future, I would continue using strategies such as requirement analysis, UML modeling, and iterative design to improve both clarity and efficiency.
